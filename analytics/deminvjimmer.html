Egor Demin vs. Jimmer Fredette

Comparing BYU’s two modern first-rounders using real rookie-season NBA performance data.

Analytical Overview

This dashboard examines early NBA performance for Egor Demin (2025–26) and Jimmer Fredette (2011–12), using game-by-game box scores to compare:

  • Scoring output and consistency over games.
  • Minutes and usage vs. per-game production.
  • Early efficiency signals (shooting splits, per-minute scoring).

Built as a Smartabase-style view: simple, actionable visuals that coaches and performance staff can scan in seconds.

Game-by-Game Points

Tracks points across each player’s rookie games. Demin’s initial sample is overlaid against Fredette’s full rookie season to contextualize his start.

Minutes vs. Points

Shows how efficiently each player converts playing time into scoring. This can highlight whether Demin is maximizing his usage.

Rolling Scoring Trend

A 3-game rolling average smooths volatility and shows how quickly each player stabilizes at the NBA level.

Data & Methods

Data sourced from public NBA box scores. Totals rows removed; minutes converted from MM:SS to decimal; core metrics standardized (PTS, MIN, FG%, 3P%, AST, REB). Visuals generated in Python and rendered with Plotly.js for this page.
